Analyzing NYC SAT Scores¶

The goal of this project is to examine the relationships between NYC SAT scores and different demographics. To accomplish this, we will use data that has been made publicly available by New York City Public Schools.

Sumary of Findings¶

We concluded that there are several factors that correlate with a school's average SAT score:

  • Schools with a higher percentage of white or asian students tend to correlate with higher SAT scores. While, schools with a higher percentage of black or hispanic students are correlated with lower SAT scores.
  • From the survey responses, perceptions on school safety by teachers and students correlated positively with SAT scores. Interestingly, parents' scores were less reliable as indicators.
  • Schools with a higher percentage of females students scored higher on the SAT.
  • Schools with a higher percentage of AP test takers also had higher SAT scores.

Read in the data¶

The following data sets will be used for this project:

  • NYC SAT Scores - Average SAT scores for each high school in New York City
  • School Attendance - Attendance and enrollment statistics broken down by school district
  • Class Size - Average class size for each school
  • AP Test Results - Advanced Placement (AP) exam results for each high school
  • Graduation Outcomes - The percentage of students who graduated
  • Demographics - Demographic information for each school
  • School Survey - Surveys of parents, teachers, and students at each school

    string_representation = str(num)

        return string_representation
    else:

def find_lat(loc):
    coords = re.findall("\(.+, .+\)", loc)
    lat = coords[0].split(",")[0].replace("(", "")
    return lat

def find_lon(loc):
    coords = re.findall("\(.+, .+\)", loc)
    lon = coords[0].split(",")[1].replace(")", "").strip()
    return lon

data["hs_directory"]["lat"] = data["hs_directory"]["Location 1"].apply(find_lat)
data["hs_directory"]["lon"] = data["hs_directory"]["Location 1"].apply(find_lon)

data["hs_directory"]["lat"] = pd.to_numeric(data["hs_directory"]["lat"], errors="coerce")
data["hs_directory"]["lon"] = pd.to_numeric(data["hs_directory"]["lon"], errors="coerce")
